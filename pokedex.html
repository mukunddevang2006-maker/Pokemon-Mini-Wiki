What is a Pokédex?


                A Pokédex, is a digital encyclopedia used in thePokémon world
                to record detailed information about different Pokémon species.
                It automatically collects data about a Pokémon when it is encountered or caught by a trainer.
                The Pokédex provides important details such as the Pokémon's name, type,
                abilities, and basic characteristics.
            
                In the Pokémon universe, the Pokédex is usually given to trainers at the 
                beginning of their journey to help them learn about Pokémon and explore the world more effectively.

Why is Pokédex important?


                The Pokédex plays a crucial role in both the Pokémon world and for trainers.
                It encourages exploration, learning, and collection by motivating trainers to encounter new Pokémon species.
                Completing the Pokédex is considered a major achievement, as it requires knowledge, strategy, and dedication.
            
    
                The Pokédex also helps trainers understand Pokémon strengths, weaknesses,
                and abilities, which is useful for battles and team building.
                Overall, it serves as a central knowledge base that supports a trainer's journey and growth.
