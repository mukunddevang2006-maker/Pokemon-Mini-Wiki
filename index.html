What is Pokémon?

                Pokémon is a popular media franchise created by Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures Inc.
                The word “Pokémon” is short for “Pocket Monsters.” 
                It revolves around fictional creatures called Pokémon, which humans, known as Pokémon Trainers, catch and train to battle each other.
            
                Pokémon first appeared as a video game series and later expanded into animated shows,movies, trading cards, and merchandise. 
                Each Pokémon has unique abilities, characteristics, and elemental types that define how it behaves and battles.
            
                Every Pokémon creature has an array of stats. HP (Hit Points) is a Pokémon's life force.
                If your Pokémon's HP hits zero, it faints and is no longer usable in battle (it can still use Hidden Machines, though). 
                The speed stat decides which Pokémon will make the first move in battle. This stat can be critical in long battles.
            
                Attack and Special Attack measure the strength of moves used by your Pokémon; the higher this is, the more damage you will do to opponents.
                Attack corresponds to moves in the Physical category, while Special Attack corresponds to Special moves.
            
                Similarly, Defense and Special Defense measure the ability to take attacks from other Pokémon; the higher the stat is, the fewer Hit Points will be lost when attacked.
                Defense corresponds to moves in the Physical category, while Special Defense corresponds to Special moves.

History of Pokémon


                    The idea behind Pokémon  was conceived by Satoshi Tajiri, who grew up in Machida, Japan.
                    As a child, Tajiri enjoyed collecting insects and small creatures in nature.
                    When urban development destroyed much of this environment, he wanted to recreate that experience in a virtual form.
                    His interest in video games, especially the Game Boy's link cable,
                    inspired the concept of trading creatures between players.
                
                    In the early 1980s, Tajiri started a self-published video game magazine called Game Freak.
                    Over time, this magazine evolved into a game development team.
                    Along with illustrator Ken Sugimori and composer Junichi Masuda, Tajiri officially founded Game Freak in 1989.
                
                    The concept of Pokémon began development in the early 1990s under the name Capsule Monsters,
                    later renamed Pocket Monsters (Pokémon).
                    The idea centered on capturing creatures, storing them in capsules, and exchanging them with others using the Game Boy link cable.
                    Nintendo supported the project,
                    and two versions of the game—Red and Green—were released in Japan on 27 February 1996.
                
                    Although initially overlooked,Pokémon slowly gained popularity through word of mouth, promotions,
                    and magazine features.
                    A major boost came from the anime series, which premiered in 1997, with Pikachu chosen as the franchise's central mascot.
                    Despite a temporary setback caused by a television incident in 1997, Pokémon recovered quickly and continued to grow.
                
                    Pokémon expanded beyond video games into trading cards, movies,merchandise,
                    and an internationally successful anime.
                    By the late 1990s, it had become a global phenomenon known as Pokémania, especially in North America and Europe.
                
                    To manage the growing franchise, The Pokémon Company was established in 2000, jointly owned by
                    Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures Inc.
                    Over the years, Pokémon continued evolving through new game generations, regions, and technologies, including fully 3D games and open-world gameplay.
                
                    In 2016, the release of Pokémon GO introduced a new worldwide craze by combining Pokémon with augmented reality.
                    Today, Pokémon remains one of the most successful and enduring entertainment franchises in history, spanning games, anime, films, cards, and global merchandise.
